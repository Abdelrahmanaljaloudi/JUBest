Basic Arabic Phrases

In this section, you’ll find essential Arabic phrases that every traveler should know by heart regardless of the reason why they’re traveling.After all, whether you’re on holiday or on business, you’ll always find yourself in situations in which you need to use the Arabic language to greet people, introduce yourself, exchange courtesies and say “sorry” and “thank you.”.

Below are a few basic phrases and expressions you could find very useful in most conversations:

Arabic Greetings

Arabic Phrases Romanizations English Translations Pronunciation of Phrases
السلام عليكم Assalamu alaikum Be upon you
وعليكم السلام Wa alaikum assalam And upon you be peace
مهلا Mahlan Hey
كيف تجري الامور Kayfa Tajri Alumur? How’s it going?
ما أخبارك؟ Ma Akhbarakura? What’s up?
سعيد بلقائك Ana Saeed Beliqa’ak (M) / Belqa’ak (F) Nice to meet you
مساء الخير Masa’o lkhayr Good evening
صباح الخير Sabaho lkhayr Good morning
أهلا Ahlan Hello
أين أنت؟ Ayna ant? Where are you?
شكرا Shukran Thanks
لا مشكلة La mushkila No problem
أراك لاحقا Araka lahikan See you soon
أنا لست مهتما Ana lastu muhtaman I’m not interested
كيف يمكنني أن…؟ Kayfa yumkinony ann…? How can I …?
ما إسمك؟ Ma usmuk? What’s your name?
إن شاء الله Insha Allah God willing
ماذا تفعله في أوقات فراغك؟ Maza taf’aluhu fi awqati faraghik? What’s you like to do in your free time?
ما هي الوظيفة التي تحلم بها؟ Ma hya lwadifato lati tahlomo biha? What’s your dream job?

Basic Arabic Phrases and Expressions for Traveling and Shopping

Below are some phrases that could be useful if you’re not native or extremely familiar with the country you’re visiting:

Arabic Phrases Romanizations English Translations Pronunciation of Phrases
من أين أنت؟ Min ayna ant? Where are you from?
أنا من… Ana min… I’m from …
بكم هذا؟ Bikam haza? How much is this?
أتحدث العربية بطلاقة La atahadathu alarabya bitalaqa I don’t speak Arabic fluently
منذ متى وأنت هنا؟ Munzu mata wa anta huna? How long have you been here?
أين يمكننا توقيف السيارات؟ Ayna yomkinona tawqifo sayarat? Where can we go hitchhiking?
فلنأكل بعض الطعام. أنا جائع. Falnakul ba’ada ta’am. Ana ja’ea Let’s have some food. I’m hungry.
ما هي أحسن الأماكن للزيارة في …؟ Ma hya ahsanu alamakini lizyarati fi …? What are the best places to visit in …?

Basic Arabic Phrases and Expressions for Emergency

Below are some phrases to use if you are not native and find yourself in an emergency situation:

Arabic Phrases Romanizations English Translations Pronunciation of Phrases
أحتاج إلى المساعدة Ahtaju ila almusa’ada I need help
أين هو المستشفى؟ Ayna hwa almustashfa? Where is the hospital?
هل لديك هاتف؟ Hal ladayka hatif? Do you have a phone?
هل يمكنك الإتصال بالشرطة؟ Hal yumkinoka alitissal bishorta? Can you call the police?
هل يمكنك الإتصال بمركز الإطفاء؟ Hal yumkinoka alitisalo bimarkazi litfa’e? Can you call the fire department?
أنا في خطر Ana fi khatar I’m in danger
فلنخرج من هنا Falnakhruj min huna Let’s get out of here

Basic Arabic Phrases and Expressions for Holidays

Below are some phrases if you want to wish someone a happy holiday or celebration:

Arabic Phrases Romanizations English Translations Pronunciation of Phrases
عيد ميلاد سعيد Ida mealadin sa’id Happy Birthday
عيد سعيد Eid Sa’id Happy Eid
كل عام وأنتم بخير Kula a’am wa antum bikhayr Happy New Year
هنيئا / مبروك Hani’an / Mabruk Congratulations
حفل زفاف سعيد Hafla zifafin sa’id Happy wedding
كعكة عيد ميلاد Ka’akato idi milad Birthday cake
